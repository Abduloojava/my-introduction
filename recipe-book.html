Jollof Rice

Ingredients

  • 3 cups Rice
  • 3 Medium Size Red Bell Peppers (Tatashe)
  • 1 can of Plum Tomatoes/ 4 Medium Tomatoes
  • 1.5 Cup Beef or Chicken Stock or Water
  • 100g Tomato Paste
  • 2 Onions
  • 150ml Vegetable Oil
  • 2 Tablespoons Butter
  • 2 Inch Root Ginger
  • 1 Tbsp Curry Powder
  • 2 Tbsp seasoning or any seasons of your choosing
  • 1.5 Tbsp White Pepper
  • 1 Tbsp Thyme
  • Salt to taste

Preparation

Firstly, wash your rice thoroughly till the water runs clean and set aside…Blend the Red Bell Peppers, Ginger, Garlic, Scotch Bonnet, Tomatoes and 1 Onion till smooth and set aside…Place a fairly big pot on medium heat. When it is hot, add the blended Pepper, Curry Powder, Thyme, Seasoning, Knorr Cubes, White Pepper, Bay leaves and salt to taste. Mix thoroughly, leave to boil for 10-12 minutes, stir occasionally. This step is to get rid of most of the water contents… If you wil be using Stock later, be careful not to add too much Salt as your Stock already contains Salt…While that is boiling, place a Saucepan on low to medium heat, add the Vegetable Oil/Cooking Oil into it, when it is hot, slice 1 Onion and pour into the Oil. Sauté for 2 minutes…Now add the Tomato Paste, fry for 10 minutes thereabouts. It is well fried when the Paste loses its sourness and begins to taste really sweet.Transfer the fried Tomato paste along with the Onions and Oil into the boiled Pepper mix and continue to fry for another 10-12 minutes. Stir every now and then…

Beans Recipe

Ingredients

  • 3 Cooking Spoons Palm Oil
  • 2 Red Bell Pepper (Tatashe)
  • 2 Tomatoes or 1/2 Can Plum Tomatoes
  • 1/2 Cup Washed and Shredded Smoked Fish or Shawa
  • 1 -2 Tablespoons Cayenne Pepper ( Ata gungun)
  • 2-3 Tablespoons Crayfish Powder
  • 2 Onions
  • 3 Cloves Garlic
  • 1 Maggi Crayfish
  • 1 Tbsp Curry Powder
  • 2 Tbsp seasoning or any seasons of your choosing
  • 1.5 Tbsp White Pepper
  • 1 Tbsp Thyme
  • Salt to taste

Preparation

Pick, then boil the beans on high heat with 1 chopped Onion, the Garlic Cloves and Salt to Taste. Boil till the beans become soft…Blend the Bell Peppers, Tomatoes and half of an onion until smooth, then set aside...While the Beans is boiling, place a saucepan on low to medium heat. Add the Palm oil. When the oil heats up, add 1/2 Chopped Onion and sauté till the Onions begin to caramelise, this should take 6-7 minutes.Now, add half of the Crayfish powder, continue to sauté for a further 2 minutes…Then add the Shawa or Smoked fish, Sauté for 2 minutes…Then add the blended Pepper…Now, add the Cayenne Pepper, the rest of the Crayfish and Maggi Crayfish..Then, continue to fry for 10 minutes. Remember to stir every now and then to prevent burning..After 10 minutes, transfer the Stew into the Beans…Reduce the heat to low, stir and combine. Taste for seasoning and adjust if necessary. Now, leave to simmer for 3 minutes. Your Nigerian Stewed Beans is ready…Enjoy with Plantain, Yam, Bread or Garri…

Chicken Recipe

Ingredients

  • 10 Cut Pieces of Chicken
  • 6 Chicken thighs
  • 2 Tablespoons Olive Oil
  • 1.5 Cup Beef or Chicken Stock or Water
  • 3 Tablespoons Honey or 1 Tablespoon Sugar
  • 2 Onions
  • 150ml Vegetable Oil
  • 2 Tablespoons Butter
  • 2 Inch Root Ginger
  • 1 Tbsp Curry Powder
  • 2 Tbsp seasoning or any seasons of your choosing
  • 1.5 Tbsp White Pepper
  • 3 Knorr Chicken Cubes
  • 1 Tbsp Thyme
  • Salt to taste

Preparation

Combine all the dry ingredients, then rub 3/4 of the spice mix all over the Chicken Thighs, making sure you reach underneath the skin as well. You can also make cuts on the Chicken to allow the seasoning penetrate deep into the chicken. When you are done, cover with clingfilm, then leave to marinate in your fridge for at least 6 hours, overnight preferably. I did not marinate mine for long cos it was an impromptu recipe and time was not on my side.Preheat your oven to 180 degrees. Now, take out the Chicken. Combine the wet ingredients, then spread all over the Chicken, then begin to massage the wet mix into the chicken, ensure the chicken is well coated with the mix. Arrange the Orange, Lemon and Onion slices on the chicken, You may place some underneath the chicken pieces as well...Place the chicken into the preheated oven, then grill or roast for 45 minutes to 1 hour. Depending on the type of pan you are using, you may wish to turn the chicken over at some point during the grilling process. I did not need to as I used a really good Cast Iron pan. Halfway into the grilling, rub some of the juice in the pan all over the chicken to prevent dryness.
