Exotic Travel - Discover Destinations Beyond the Ordinary

Looking for an exotic locale off the usual tourist map? Nigeria delivers. Ride the waves at Bar Beach Lagos, swim year-round in Wikki Warm Spring at Yankari Game Reserve Bauchi, take sunset boat rides at Jabi Lake Abuja, trek forest trails at Ugudoro Ranch Calabar, or walk 15th-century alleys at Kurmi Market Kano. Each spot gives you a different side of Nigeria — from Atlantic coast to northern savanna, city lakes to heritage markets. Pick your locale and go.

A place to visits

  1. Bar Beach Lagos Nigeria

    2. Bar Beach Lagos is the city’s most famous beachfront along Ahmadu Bello Way, Victoria Island. Known for Atlantic waves, sunset views, horse rides, and street food like suya and roasted corn. Swimming is risky due to strong currents, so most visitors go for evening walks and Lagos beach culture. Part of the shoreline was reclaimed for Eko Atlantic City.

  2. Ugudoro Ranch Calabar Nigeria

    3. Ugudoro Ranch is a hidden eco-tourism spot near Calabar, Cross River State. Its a mix of farmland, wildlife, and forest trails. Visitors go for bird watching, horse riding, picnic spots, and fresh farm produce. Its quieter than Obudu Mountain Resort but gives you a real taste of Calabar is green, rural side.

  3. Yankari Game Reserve Bauchi Nigeria

    4. Yankari National Park in Bauchi is Nigeria’s top wildlife safari destination. Home to elephants, baboons, hippos, roan antelopes, and over 350 bird species. The main highlight is the Wikki Warm Spring — a natural pool with 31°C clear water perfect for swimming year-round. Stay in lodges inside the park for full bush experience.

  4. Jabi Lake Park Abuja Nigeria

    5. Jabi Lake Park is Abuja is go-to spot for leisure + recreation. Its a man-made lake on the city edge with boat rides, jet skiing, horse riding, restaurants, and open lawns for picnics. Great for sunset views and weekend hangouts. Unlike Bar Beach Lagos, the water is calm so water sports are safe here.

  5. Kurmi Market Kano Nigeria

    6. Kurmi Market is one of West Africas oldest markets, right in the heart of Kano City. Founded in the 15th century, its a maze of stalls selling leather goods, textiles, spices, and Hausa crafts. Its more culture + history than beach, but it gives you the real Kano trading vibe. A must-visit for travelers into heritage tourism.
